# Enforcement and Penalties: NDPA vs GDPR

**Reference:** Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA) | EU General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679 (GDPR)
**Prepared by:** TrustGrid Technology

---

## Overview

Both the NDPA and GDPR have moved beyond theoretical frameworks into active enforcement. The regulators under both frameworks have demonstrated willingness to investigate, sanction, and fine organisations that fail to meet their obligations.

This document covers the enforcement mechanisms, penalty structures, and real world enforcement actions under both frameworks.

---

## Regulatory Authorities

### NDPA: Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC)

The NDPC is the primary enforcement authority for the NDPA. It was established as an independent commission with broad investigative and enforcement powers.

**Key powers of the NDPC:**
- Investigate complaints from data subjects
- Conduct compliance audits of organisations
- Issue enforcement notices requiring corrective action
- Impose administrative fines
- Refer serious violations for criminal prosecution
- Maintain the register of licensed DPCOs
- Issue guidance and regulatory directives

**How to contact the NDPC:**
Website: ndpc.gov.ng
Location: Abuja, Nigeria

### GDPR: Data Protection Authorities (DPAs)

GDPR enforcement is carried out by national Data Protection Authorities in each of the 27 EU member states. These authorities cooperate through the European Data Protection Board (EDPB) for cross-border cases.

**Key EU DPAs:**
| Country | Authority | Website |
|---|---|---|
| Ireland | Data Protection Commission (DPC) | dataprotection.ie |
| Germany | Bundesbeauftragter für den Datenschutz (BfDI) | bfdi.bund.de |
| France | Commission Nationale de l'Informatique et des Libertés (CNIL) | cnil.fr |
| Netherlands | Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (AP) | autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl |
| UK (post-Brexit) | Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) | ico.org.uk |

**Note:** The UK is no longer an EU member state. UK GDPR is a separate framework that closely mirrors EU GDPR but is enforced by the ICO under UK law.

## Penalty Structures

### NDPA Penalties

| Violation Type | Maximum Penalty |
|---|---|
| Administrative fine | ₦10,000,000 or 2% of annual gross revenue, whichever is greater |
| Criminal liability | Possible for serious violations, prosecuted through Nigerian courts |
| Enforcement notice | Regulator can require corrective action within a specified timeframe |
| Audit requirement | Regulator can mandate a compliance audit at the organisation's expense |

**How the fine is calculated:**
The NDPC applies whichever is greater between the flat ₦10 million fine and 2% of annual gross revenue. For large organisations this means the revenue-based calculation will almost always produce the higher penalty.

### GDPR Penalties

| Violation Category | Examples | Maximum Fine |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (less serious) | Failure to maintain records, failure to notify breach to DPA, failure to appoint DPO | €10,000,000 or 2% of global annual turnover |
| Tier 2 (most serious) | Violation of basic processing principles, violation of data subject rights, unlawful international transfers | €20,000,000 or 4% of global annual turnover |

**How the fine is calculated:**
GDPR fines are based on global annual turnover not just revenue from the EU. For multinational organisations this significantly increases the potential fine. The €20 million ceiling is only a ceiling for smaller organisations. For large multinationals the 4% of global turnover calculation produces much higher penalties.

## How Investigations Are Triggered

### Under the NDPA

Investigations by the NDPC are typically triggered by:
- Complaints from data subjects whose rights have been violated
- Data breach notifications submitted to the NDPC
- Failure to file annual Compliance Audit Returns
- Proactive regulatory sweeps of specific sectors
- Referrals from other Nigerian regulatory bodies such as the CBN or FCCPC
- Media reports of data protection violations
- Whistleblower reports from employees or former employees

### Under the GDPR

Investigations by EU DPAs are typically triggered by:
- Complaints from data subjects
- Data breach notifications submitted to the DPA
- Referrals from other EU DPAs through the cooperation mechanism
- Proactive investigations into specific sectors or practices
- Media reports and investigative journalism
- Whistleblower reports
- Complaints from consumer organisations and civil society groups

## Factors That Affect Penalty Severity

Both the NDPA and GDPR provide regulators with discretion in determining the appropriate penalty. Factors that typically increase or decrease the severity of a fine include:

### Factors That Increase Penalties
- Intentional or negligent violation rather than accidental
- History of previous violations
- Large number of data subjects affected
- Sensitive categories of data involved
- Failure to cooperate with the regulator during investigation
- Financial gain obtained as a result of the violation
- Failure to implement basic security measures

### Factors That Reduce Penalties
- Prompt self-reporting of the violation
- Full cooperation with the regulator
- Proactive steps taken to mitigate harm to data subjects
- Evidence of a mature compliance program even if imperfect
- No previous violations
- Prompt implementation of corrective measures
- Early payment of fines where applicable

## Enforcement Trends to Watch

### NDPA Enforcement Trends 2026

- The NDPC is actively reviewing the NDPA to incorporate AI-specific provisions following the June 2026 announcement. Organisations using AI for automated decision making, profiling, or high-risk processing should expect stricter scrutiny.
- Fintech and financial services remain a priority enforcement sector given the volume of personal and financial data processed.
- The NDPC is increasing coordination with other Nigerian regulators including the CBN and FCCPC creating the potential for multi-regulator enforcement actions.
- Annual CAR filing compliance is being actively monitored. Organisations that have not filed are at increasing risk of proactive investigation.

### GDPR Enforcement Trends 2026

- Cookie consent and tracking technologies remain a top enforcement priority across multiple EU DPAs.
- AI systems and automated decision making are receiving increased regulatory attention following EU AI Act implementation.
- Cross-border data transfers remain a major enforcement area particularly for transfers to the United States.
- Children's data protection is a growing enforcement priority.
- Smaller organisations are increasingly being fined as DPAs extend enforcement beyond large technology companies.

## What to Do If You Receive a Regulatory Inquiry

### Under the NDPA
1. Do not ignore the inquiry. Failure to respond is itself a violation.
2. Engage legal counsel with NDPA expertise immediately.
3. Cooperate fully with the NDPC investigation.
4. Preserve all relevant documentation and do not destroy records.
5. Conduct an internal review to understand the scope of any potential violation.
6. Consider proactive self-disclosure if you identify violations before the regulator does.

### Under the GDPR
1. Acknowledge receipt of the inquiry within the required timeframe.
2. Engage legal counsel with GDPR expertise immediately.
3. Cooperate fully with the DPA investigation.
4. Preserve all relevant documentation.
5. Conduct an internal review of the alleged violation.
6. Consider whether the violation requires notification to affected data subjects.
