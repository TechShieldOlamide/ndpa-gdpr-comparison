# When Does Each Law Apply?

**Reference:** Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA) | EU General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679 (GDPR)
**Prepared by:** TrustGrid Technology

---

## The Core Question

Before you build a compliance program, you need to answer one question clearly:

Which law applies to my business?

The answer is not always one or the other. For many African businesses operating in the global digital economy, the answer is both. At the same time. With different regulators watching.

---

## When the NDPA Applies

The NDPA 2023 applies to your business if you:

| Scenario | NDPA Applies? |
|---|---|
| Are incorporated in Nigeria | ✅ Yes |
| Have a branch, office, or representative in Nigeria | ✅ Yes |
| Process personal data of individuals residing in Nigeria from outside Nigeria | ✅ Yes |
| Offer goods or services to individuals in Nigeria, even for free | ✅ Yes |
| Monitor the behaviour of individuals located in Nigeria | ✅ Yes |
| Are a foreign company with no Nigerian presence but have Nigerian users | ✅ Yes |
| Process only the data of non-Nigerian individuals outside Nigeria | ❌ No |

### Practical Examples

**Example 1:** A UK-based SaaS company offers a project management tool used by Nigerian businesses. Nigerian employees sign up and use the platform. The NDPA applies to how that company handles those Nigerian users' data even though the company has no office in Nigeria.

**Example 2:** A Lagos-based fintech processes payment data for Nigerian customers only. The NDPA applies. GDPR does not apply unless the fintech also serves EU residents.

**Example 3:** A Nigerian developer builds an app used exclusively by users in Germany and France. The NDPA may not apply to those EU users but GDPR does.

---

## When the GDPR Applies

The GDPR applies to your business if you:

| Scenario | GDPR Applies? |
|---|---|
| Are established in an EU member state | ✅ Yes |
| Process personal data of EU residents while offering them goods or services | ✅ Yes |
| Monitor the behaviour of individuals located in the EU | ✅ Yes |
| Are established outside the EU but target EU users | ✅ Yes |
| Process only non-EU residents' data with no EU establishment | ❌ No |

### Practical Examples

**Example 1:** A Nigerian fintech launches a product available to users in the UK and Germany. The GDPR applies to how it handles those users' data even though the company is based in Lagos.

**Example 2:** A Nigerian e-commerce platform runs targeted ads at French consumers and allows them to purchase products. GDPR applies.

**Example 3:** An Abuja-based consulting firm serves only Nigerian government clients. GDPR does not apply.

---

## When Both Apply Simultaneously

This is the situation most Nigerian digital businesses with global ambitions will find themselves in.

Both the NDPA and GDPR apply simultaneously when your business:

| Scenario | Both Apply? |
|---|---|
| Is based in Nigeria and has customers in EU member states | ✅ Yes |
| Processes Nigerian and EU residents' data on the same platform | ✅ Yes |
| Transfers personal data between Nigeria and the EU | ✅ Yes |
| Uses EU-based cloud infrastructure to process Nigerian user data | ✅ Yes |
| Has Nigerian staff and EU customers | ✅ Yes |

### What Dual Application Means in Practice

When both laws apply you do not get to choose which one to follow. You must satisfy both regulators simultaneously.

Where the two frameworks have different requirements, you must meet the higher standard to remain compliant under both.

Where the frameworks conflict, you need legal guidance on how to navigate the conflict in your specific jurisdiction and business context.
