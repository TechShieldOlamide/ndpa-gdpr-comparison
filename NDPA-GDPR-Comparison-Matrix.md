# NDPA vs GDPR Comparison Matrix

**Reference:** Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 (NDPA) | EU General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679 (GDPR)
**Last Updated:** 2026
**Prepared by:** TrustGrid Technology

---

## How to Read This Matrix

This matrix compares the NDPA and GDPR across their major provisions. For each topic the matrix shows:
- What the NDPA requires
- What the GDPR requires
- The practical implication for a business operating under both

**Compliance Status Key**
- ✅ Both frameworks align closely
- ⚠️ Frameworks differ, additional action required
- ❌ Significant gap, careful attention needed

---

## 1. Territorial Scope

| Aspect | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Geographic reach | Applies to any organisation processing personal data of individuals residing in Nigeria, regardless of where the organisation is located | Applies to any organisation processing personal data of EU residents, regardless of where the organisation is located | ✅ Both have extraterritorial reach | A Nigerian fintech with EU customers must comply with both simultaneously |
| Physical presence required | No. Applies based on data subjects' location | No. Applies based on data subjects' location | ✅ | Operating online does not exempt you from either framework |
| Applies to foreign companies | Yes. Any company processing Nigerian residents' data | Yes. Any company processing EU residents' data | ✅ | Foreign SaaS platforms serving Nigerian or EU users must comply |

---

## 2. Regulatory Authority

| Aspect | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary regulator | Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) | Data Protection Authorities (DPAs) in each EU member state e.g. ICO in UK, CNIL in France, BfDI in Germany | ⚠️ | GDPR has 27+ national regulators. NDPA has one central regulator. Businesses must identify which EU DPA leads for their operations |
| Regulator independence | NDPC is an independent commission established by the NDPA | DPAs are independent public authorities | ✅ | Both regulators operate independently of government interference |
| Cross-border enforcement | NDPC handles all Nigerian enforcement | EU DPAs cooperate through the European Data Protection Board (EDPB) | ⚠️ | EU enforcement can be coordinated across multiple countries simultaneously |

## 3. Definition of Personal Data

| Aspect | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Core definition | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person | ✅ | Definitions are substantially aligned |
| Examples covered | Names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, IP addresses, biometric data, financial data, BVN | Names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, IP addresses, biometric data, financial data, location data | ✅ | Both frameworks cover digital identifiers including IP addresses and device IDs |
| Special categories | Health data, biometric data, genetic data, racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, criminal records | Health data, biometric data, genetic data, racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, sexual orientation, criminal records | ⚠️ | GDPR explicitly includes sexual orientation as a special category. Check your data inventory against both lists |

## 4. Lawful Basis for Processing

| Aspect | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Consent | Valid, freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent | Valid, freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent | ✅ | Consent requirements are closely aligned |
| Contract | Processing necessary for the performance of a contract | Processing necessary for the performance of a contract | ✅ | Same basis applies under both frameworks |
| Legal obligation | Processing required by Nigerian law | Processing required by EU member state law or EU law | ✅ | Each framework recognises legal obligations under its own jurisdiction |
| Legitimate interests | Recognised as a lawful basis with balancing test required | Recognised as a lawful basis with balancing test required | ✅ | Both require documenting the legitimate interest and balancing it against data subject rights |
| Vital interests | Recognised | Recognised | ✅ | |
| Public task | Recognised for public authorities | Recognised for public authorities | ✅ | |
| Number of lawful bases | 6 | 6 | ✅ | Both frameworks provide the same number of lawful bases |

## 5. Data Subject Rights

| Right | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Right to be informed | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ | Privacy notices required under both frameworks |
| Right of access | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ | Individuals can request a copy of their data under both |
| Right to rectification | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ | Individuals can correct inaccurate data under both |
| Right to erasure | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ | Right to be forgotten exists under both frameworks |
| Right to restrict processing | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ | |
| Right to data portability | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ | Both require data in structured, machine-readable format |
| Right to object | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ | Including objection to direct marketing |
| Rights related to automated decision making | ✅ Yes, Section 37 | ✅ Yes, Article 22 | ✅ | Both grant rights not to be subject to solely automated decisions with significant effects |
| Response timeframe | Not explicitly specified in days, must be without undue delay | 1 calendar month, extendable by 2 months for complex requests | ⚠️ | GDPR is more prescriptive on timeframes. Apply the 30-day GDPR standard to satisfy both |

## 6. Data Protection Officer (DPO)

| Aspect | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| When required | Data controllers of major importance processing data of more than 200 data subjects within 6 months | Required when processing is carried out by a public authority, when core activities involve large scale systematic monitoring, or when core activities involve large scale processing of special category data | ⚠️ | NDPA threshold is lower and more broadly applicable. Most Nigerian businesses processing customer data will need a DPO |
| DPO qualifications | Must have professional knowledge of data protection law and practice | Must have expert knowledge of data protection law and practice | ✅ | |
| DPO independence | Must be able to perform duties independently | Must be able to perform duties independently | ✅ | |
| DPO registration | Must be registered with the NDPC | Must be notified to the relevant DPA in some EU member states | ⚠️ | NDPA requires formal NDPC registration of the DPO |

## 7. Data Breach Notification

| Aspect | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Notification to regulator required | Yes | Yes, when breach is likely to result in risk to individuals | ⚠️ | GDPR has a risk threshold. NDPA requires notification for all breaches |
| Timeframe for regulator notification | 72 hours | 72 hours | ✅ | Same timeframe under both frameworks |
| Notification to affected individuals | Required when breach is likely to result in high risk | Required when breach is likely to result in high risk to individuals | ✅ | |
| Timeframe for individual notification | Without undue delay | Without undue delay | ✅ | |
| Breach register required | Yes | Yes | ✅ | Maintain an internal log of all breaches regardless of whether notification is required |

## 8. Cross-Border Data Transfers

| Aspect | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Transfers restricted by default | Yes | Yes | ✅ | Both frameworks restrict transfers to countries without adequate protection |
| Adequacy decisions | NDPC maintains a whitelist of adequate countries | European Commission issues adequacy decisions | ⚠️ | Different whitelists. A country adequate under GDPR may not be adequate under NDPA and vice versa |
| Standard Contractual Clauses | Recognised as a transfer mechanism | Recognised as a transfer mechanism (SCCs) | ✅ | SCCs can be used under both frameworks but must meet each framework's requirements |
| Binding Corporate Rules | Not explicitly addressed in NDPA | Recognised for intra-group transfers | ❌ | GDPR BCRs do not automatically satisfy NDPA transfer requirements |
| Transfer impact assessments | Required where standard clauses are used | Required where SCCs are used (Transfer Impact Assessment) | ✅ | |

## 9. Compliance Audit Requirements

| Aspect | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mandatory audit | Yes. Annual Compliance Audit Return (CAR) must be filed with NDPC by 31 March each year | No mandatory annual audit filing but accountability principle requires ongoing compliance documentation | ⚠️ | NDPA is more prescriptive. Nigerian businesses must file annual audit returns with the NDPC |
| Who must file | Data controllers and processors of major importance (DCPMIs) | All data controllers and processors | ⚠️ | GDPR applies to all processors. NDPA audit filing obligation applies to DCPMIs specifically |
| Licensed compliance organisations | Must engage a licensed Data Protection Compliance Organisation (DPCO) for audit | No equivalent requirement | ❌ | NDPA requires use of NDPC-licensed DPCOs. No equivalent exists under GDPR |

## 10. Penalties and Enforcement

| Aspect | NDPA 2023 | GDPR | Alignment | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Maximum fine | ₦10 million or 2% of annual gross revenue, whichever is greater | Up to €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover for the most serious violations, whichever is greater | ⚠️ | GDPR fines are significantly higher in absolute terms. Both use revenue-based calculations |
| Tiered penalties | Single tier under NDPA | Two tiers: up to €10M/2% for less serious violations and up to €20M/4% for most serious violations | ⚠️ | GDPR has more granular penalty structure |
| Criminal liability | Possible for serious violations | Possible under national implementing laws in EU member states | ⚠️ | Criminal liability exists under both but varies by jurisdiction |
| Enforcement track record | NDPC has imposed significant fines including ₦766.2 million against Multichoice Nigeria | EU DPAs have imposed billions in fines including €1.2 billion against Meta Ireland | ✅ | Both regulators are actively enforcing. Neither framework is theoretical |
